Discover Syria

Historical Sites

Damascus Citadel

A grand defensive fortress in the heart of Damascus, rebuilt during the Ayyubid era, playing a vital defensive role through history.

Saint Simeon Church

A great Byzantine church in the Aleppo countryside, dating back to the 5th century AD, built in honor of Saint Simeon Stylites.

Palmyra

The Bride of the Syrian Desert, a major trade hub on the Silk Road, famous for its temples and grand colonnades.

Aleppo Citadel

One of the largest and oldest citadels in the world, famous for its massive architecture and ancient history.

Natural Wonders

Darkush

A peaceful town on the Orontes River, known for its greenery and fresh springs. A perfect spot for relaxation.

Latakia Beach

One of Syria’s finest beaches, with clear waters and golden sands. Ideal for swimming and relaxation.

Euphrates River

A historic river flowing through eastern Syria, nourishing farmlands and holding deep cultural significance.

Barada Valley

A green valley near Damascus, famous for its fresh springs and beautiful nature, especially in spring.

Religious Sites

Umayyad Mosque

One of the oldest and most significant mosques in the world, featuring unique architecture and rich history in the heart of Damascus.

Mar Takla Monastery

A historic monastery in the Qalamoun mountains, a symbol of religious coexistence, preserving the Aramaic language to this day.

Mosque of Binnish

A key religious and social center in Binnish, known for its tall minaret and spacious courtyard.

Mariamite Church

One of the oldest churches in Old Damascus, serving as an important spiritual center for the Orthodox Christian community.

Syrian Foods

Kibbeh

Kibbeh

Kibbeh stuffed with meat and pine nuts, grilled or fried.

Mansaf

Mansaf

Meat served with rice and fermented yogurt sauce, a famous dish in southern Syria.

Falafel

Falafel

Fried balls made from chickpeas or fava beans with special spices, usually served with bread and tahini sauce.

Shawarma

Shawarma

Seasoned meat roasted on a vertical spit and thinly sliced, served in bread with tahini and fresh vegetables.
